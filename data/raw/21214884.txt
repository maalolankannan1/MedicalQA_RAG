Can 'high-risk' human papillomaviruses (HPVs) be detected in human breast milk?

Mammas IN(1), Zaravinos A, Sourvinos G, Myriokefalitakis N, Theodoridou M, 
Spandidos DA.

Author information:
(1)Department of Paediatrics, 'Penteli' Children's Hospital, Athens, Greece. 
mammasjo@googlemail.com

AIM: Human papillomavirus (HPV) transmission via nonsexual modes in childhood 
has been proposed by several researchers. The aim of our study was to determine 
the presence of 'high-risk' HPV DNA in human breast milk.
METHODS: Using polymerase chain reaction techniques, we evaluated the presence 
of HPV infection in human breast milk collected from 21 HPV-positive and 11 
HPV-negative mothers.
RESULTS: Of the 32 studied human milk specimens, no 'high-risk' HPV 16, 18, 31, 
33, 35, 39, 45, 51, 52, 56, 58 or 58 DNA was detected.
CONCLUSION: This preliminary case-control study indicates the absence of mucosal 
'high-risk' HPV types in human breast milk.
