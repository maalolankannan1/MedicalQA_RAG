1. Dentomaxillofac Radiol. 1997 Jan;26(1):67-9. doi: 10.1038/sj.dmfr.4600204.

Can dentists recognize manipulated digital radiographs?

Visser H(1), Krüger W.

Author information:
(1)Department of Periodontology, Dental School, Georg-August-University 
Göttingen, Germany.

OBJECTIVES: To determine the ability of dentists to recognize digitally 
manipulated radiographs.
METHODS: A poster was presented at the Annual Meeting of the German Society for 
Periodontology displaying the intra-oral radiographs of 12 different patients. 
Half of the radiographs were subjected to digital manipulation to add or remove 
specific features. Dentists were asked to identify these radiographs by means of 
a questionnaire.
RESULTS: Thirty-nine dentists submitted usable questionnaires. Statistical 
evaluation revealed a distribution of hits similar to the random distribution. 
None of the dentists detected all the six manipulated radiographs; three 
dentists had five correct, but there were five with only one. An authentic 
radiograph scored highest as a manipulation.
CONCLUSIONS: Image manipulations which alter the diagnostic content of a 
radiograph are unlikely to be detected by dentists. Digital radiography requires 
additional measures for data protection.
