[Is ultrasound equal to X-ray in pediatric fracture diagnosis?].

[Article in German]

Moritz JD(1), Hoffmann B, Meuser SH, Sehr DH, Caliebe A, Heller M.

Author information:
(1)Klinik für Diagnostische Radiologie, Pädiatrische Radiologie und Sonografie, 
UK-SH, Campus Kiel. joerg.d.moritz@rad.uni-kiel.de

PURPOSE: Ultrasound is currently not established for the diagnosis of fractures. 
The aim of this study was to compare ultrasound and X-ray beyond their use 
solely for the identification of fractures, i. e., for the detection of fracture 
type and dislocation for pediatric fracture diagnosis.
MATERIALS AND METHODS: Limb bones of dead young pigs served as a model for 
pediatric bones. The fractured bones were examined with ultrasound, X-ray, and 
CT, which served as the gold standard.
RESULTS: 162 of 248 bones were fractured. 130 fractures were identified using 
ultrasound, and 148 using X-ray. There were some advantages of X-ray over 
ultrasound in the detection of fracture type (80 correct results using X-ray, 66 
correct results using ultrasound). Ultrasound, however, was superior to X-ray 
for dislocation identification (41 correct results using X-ray, 51 correct 
results using ultrasound). Both findings were not statistically significant 
after adjustment for multiple testing.
CONCLUSION: Ultrasound not only has comparable sensitivity to that of X-ray for 
the identification of limb fractures but is also equally effective for the 
diagnosis of fracture type and dislocation. Thus, ultrasound can be used as an 
adequate alternative method to X-ray for pediatric fracture diagnosis.
