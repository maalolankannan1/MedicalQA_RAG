1. Adv Clin Exp Med. 2012 Jul-Aug;21(4):469-75.

Can we predict urinary stone composition based on an analysis of microelement 
concentration in the hair and urine?

Słojewski M(1), Safranow K, Jakubowska K, Olszewska M, Pawlik A, Juzyszyn Z, 
Droździk M, Chlubek D, Hammerer P, Sikorski A.

Author information:
(1)Department of Urology and Urological Oncology, Pomeranian Medical University, 
Szczecin, Poland. mslojewski@csv.pl

BACKGROUND: In recent years the role of trace elements in lithogenesis has 
received steadily increasing attention.
OBJECTIVES: This study was aimed to attempt to find the correlations between the 
chemical content of the stones and the concentration of chosen elements in the 
urine and hair of stone formers.
MATERIAL AND METHODS: The proposal for the study was approved by the local 
ethics committee. Specimens were taken from 219 consecutive stone-formers. The 
content of the stone was evaluated using atomic absorption spectrometry, 
spectrophotometry, and colorimetric methods. An analysis of 29 elements in hair 
and 21 elements in urine was performed using inductively coupled plasma-atomic 
emission spectrometry.
RESULTS: Only a few correlations between the composition of stones and the 
distribution of elements in urine and in hair were found. All were considered 
incidental.
CONCLUSIONS: The data obtained did not allow for the creation of a proper and 
practical algorithm to predict stone chemical composition based on hair and 
urine analysis.
